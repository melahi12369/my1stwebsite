History of the Betta fish

The betta fish, or Siamese fighting fish, is a freshwater fish that is native to Southeast Asia. The betta fish is a part of of the genus Betta. This genus includes 73 species! Betta fish were first domesticated in Thailand around 1,000 years ago. Unfortunately, many betta fish were raised as fighting fish, hence their other alias. They are also known as the "designer fish of the aquatic world," because of their wide variation in colors and patterns.

Betta Fish Behavior and Temperament

Betta fish are known for being very aggressive and territorial fish. If kept in the same tank, males will fight with each other until one or both of the fishes die. Females also become territorial in confined tanks where they can't avoid each other. Betta fish like to live in larger aquariums where they can be free to roam and explore, as they are very curious and active fish. Betta fish can even get depressed if not in the right situation! Betta fish have even been shown to be capable of associative learning. This means that they will consistently react to stimuli in a predictable way, which means it is learning. If you want your little fish friend to have other tankmates, make sure they are fishes that school, like shoals and tetras.

Caring for your Betta Fish

To start, one of the most important things to take into account for the care of your betta is making sure it has a large enough tank. Many people will keep them in bowls or even cups! This will possible make your betta fish depressed and less healthy. These fish are very active and curious, so they need a big area to explore. The ideal temperature for betta is 75-78 degrees fahrenheit. The water also needs to be kept clean, as betta fish are susceptible to diseases such as fin rot, white spot, or dropsy. With betta fish, you need to make sure to decorate the tank with plants, rocks, caves, and other ornaments. This will serve to stimulate them and give them places to hide. They can also build their bubble nests on these formations. Betta fish need diets high in protein, which will keep them full and healthy. Betta fish should be kept in their tanks alone, as other betta can irritate and even egg each other on to fight.

Breeding Betta Fish

If you want to breed betta fish, there are a couple things to keep in mind. You can tell if a male is interested in a female if the male starts flaring their gills and start a doing a swimming motion that is akin to a dance. If the female is interested, she will darken in color and develop what are called "breeding bars." They will do what is called embracing, with the female releasing eggs which the male will fertilize in the water. Males will also build bubble nests for the eggs. After the female lays all her eggs, she must be removed from the tank. Females will usually eat the eggs she laid, so if left in the tank, she will either eat all the eggs, or the male will kill the female to stop her from eating the eggs. The eggs will remain in the males care until they hatch.
